Running Augmented Reality (AR) on a mobile device is notoriously brutal on the hardware. It heavily taxes the CPU (tracking the world), the GPU (rendering the camera feed and 3D objects), and the battery, leading to thermal throttling where the phone gets hot and the framerate drops.
Here is exactly why your specific architecture will withstand that stress without crashing or stuttering:
1. Zero Mid-Game Garbage Collection (GC) Mobile CPUs hate the C# Garbage Collector. When the GC runs, the entire game freezes for a few milliseconds, which in VR/AR causes immediate motion sickness. Because we enforced strict Object Pooling (pre-warming everything) and upgraded all your Event Bus payloads to readonly structs, your game allocates virtually zero memory on the Heap during combat. The Garbage Collector will stay fast asleep.
2. The Central Tick & Throttling Having 50 enemies each running their own Update() method melts mobile CPUs. By using the ITickable central loop, you have one single update method. Furthermore, because you throttle heavy calculations (like your Swarm Ants only calculating NavMesh paths twice a second instead of 60 times a second), you are saving massive amounts of CPU cycles, keeping the phone cool.
3. Background Threading Procedurally generating a 3D floor mesh requires a lot of math. If you did that on the main thread, the AR camera feed would freeze and crash the tracking. Because you offloaded the GenerateFloorMeshAsync method to a background thread using Task.Run(), the game will remain silky smooth while the phone's secondary cores do the heavy lifting in the background.
4. Decoupling & Error Safety Because everything communicates blindly through the EventBus, if a system fails or is missing (like if the user denies microphone permissions for your bubbles), the game won't throw a cascading NullReferenceException and crash. The event just fires into the void, and the game keeps running smoothly.
